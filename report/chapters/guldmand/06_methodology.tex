\paragraph{State space} The state $s_t \in \mathcal{S}$ encodes the information
available to the agent at decision time $t$. It comprises the normalized price
window, technical indicators, and the current portfolio allocation, so that
$s_t = (\mathbf{p}_{t-w:t}, \mathbf{x}_{t-1})$ where $w$ is the lookback length.

\paragraph{Action space} The action $a_t \in \mathcal{A}$ is the discrete trading
decision applied to each asset, drawn from $\{\textsc{sell}, \textsc{hold},
\textsc{buy}\}$ at fixed position sizes.

\paragraph{Reward design} 
The reward $r_t$ is the realized log-return of the
portfolio over the holding period, optionally adjusted by a risk penalty.
